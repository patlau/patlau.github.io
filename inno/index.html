Lauper IT-Consulting

Some tips on how to stay current with technology. See links for more informations.

Release notes
Read the release notes of libraries and 3rd party products you use. They are a good source for new features that you can use. You might be supprised how often libraries are just updated to new versions without making use of new features.
Projects
Monitor what projects in your company do and try to leverage usefull innovations to other projects.
Employees and co-workers
Have employees share knowledge and experience. Organize tech meetings or workshops, enable ad hoc discussions.
Consultants
Invite consultants or meet them and ask about their knowledge and experience. Follow their blogs and news. A good starting point is the thoughtworks technology radar
Conferences, workshops, events, meetups and user groups
Visit conferences and workshops like JUGS or Workshoptage. Also some companies like Microsoft offer free workshops.
Partners
Follow news and blogs of (technology) partners you already work with or 3rd party products you already use. Make use of trainings that partner offers, visit events they organize, invite consultants./
Industry standards and initiatives
Follow industry standards like Eclipse, Spring, Apache, Cloud Native Computing Foundationn, ITIL and so on
Industry leaders
Follow industry leaders like Google, Microsoft, Oracle, JBoss, VMWare, ...
Leaders
Follow technology leaders like Martin Fowler.
News portals
Read news portals, see our Links.
Read magazines
Read magazines, see our Links.
Security Standards
Follow security standards like OWASP top 10.
Universities and Training
Participate with (local) universities, see what trainings are offered, visit their events or enrol in a MAS, CAS or other course.
Customers
Communicate with customers, visit them personally, invite them to events, have a KYC (Know-Your-Customer) initiative.
Tenders and job offers
Interested what other companies are using? Check their open jobs. Take requirements and questions from tenders or offers seriously, most probably they are there for some reason, try to understand them.
